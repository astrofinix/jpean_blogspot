Simple Ways to a Self-Care Routine: A Guide to Get You Started.
Being able to take care of it is one of the best forms of self-love we can do for ourselves. Further, improving bit by bit is key in creating large changes and appreciation with your body.

Here are four main components from which you can improve: your skincare routine, showering habits, sleep, and exercise.

Here are the following easy to follow tips that I have learned from reading medical journals, and following medical practitioners to guide you, from which they have guided me into building my own routine.
1.1. Skincare Routine

General Rule of Thumb

I will not list the exact products I use but only the active ingredients that are on it. This is to ensure you find a product that works for you and simplify your routine away from being too complex. I recommend Skin Carisma and INCI Decoder for checking the safety and effectiveness of products you use and you consider buying. These two sites have one of the reputable sources for both really ineffective and highly effective skincare products.

Also, expensive does not always mean effective, the simpler your routine is the better it is.

Simply put, this routine is for morning:
  1. Cleanser
  2. Sunscreen
  3. Spot Treatment (optional)
And, this is for evening:
  1. Cleanser
  2. Retinol
  3. Spot Treatment (optional)

Cleanser

Everyday, our skin is constantly bombarded with dirt, sweat, and pollutants so cleansing it twice a day, morning and evening, is vital in maintaining the healthy look of it. Ingredients to consider are vitamin C, ceramides, retinol, salicylic acid,and glycerin among others.

Day: Sunscreen

This is by far what dermatologists strongly advise to always use when going outside even if it's a cloudy day. Sunscreen protects the skin from premature aging and adding to that there is no such thing as a healthy tan. Dermatologists stress that tanning is a way for the skin to protect itself by destroying it, and mostly one of the leading risks of it is skin cancer.

As a rule of thumb a good sunscreen has both SPF30+ and PA+++ or better (SPF 50 & PA++++).

For PA+++, it protects the skin from UVB rays, these rays are still present even on cloudy days.

For SPF, it protects the skin from UVA rays, these rays are the one responsible for having a tan and are most powerful on a clear day.

Night: Retinol

Retinol and its derivatives like retinoic acid unclogs pores, is a potent anti-aging product that reduces fine lines and any signs of premature aging

Moisturizer? Should you use one?

In hot and humid climates, like the Philippines, moisturizer is not essential since the skin barrier automatically absorbs water in the atmosphere. However, moisturizer will be needed if someone is prone to dry skin or lives in a cold climate, like Baguio. This is because since the air is not humid, thus, no water the skin does not trap water, but rather releases it. Therefore, a moisturizer is needed to trap it and preserve the skin barrier, which gives the skin a plush and healthy look.

Spot treatments

These are a product recommendation for treating specific skin concerns which have specialized ingredients.

For acne, erythromycin is a good antibacterial in treating it, as well as benzoyl peroxide.

For blackheads and whiteheads, salicylic acid is a powerful ingredient that deeply cleans the pores and declogs it of debris.

For light chemical peeling, which renews your skin, a peeling solution is applied once a week containing AHA/BHA acids such as lactic, glycolic, mandelic, and salicylic acid helps in overall cell turnover, revealing new and youthful skin.
1.2. Showering

Now, the following may cause some debate but doing the bare minimum for yourself of showering is more than enough. Moreover, if you are sweaty from an intense activity like an exercise, or just have a bad odor from the day, that simply calls for a shower. But if you just did no sweat, and just no smell, there's no mandatory rule to shower.

Furthermore, a 4-5 minute of showering with focus on cleaning your groin and armpit area will be as good as cleaning your whole body. Moreover, having fine hair, or simply having itchy scalp calls for shampooing, if not, or just having more curly hair, you can just go by not showering. Just in general, listen to your body and what it needs, briefer or lesser times of showering does not mean it's not as good as showering frequently.
1.3. Sleep

Sleep is powerful. And perhaps, the one that is being left for granted the most. Mastering your sleep and clearing some misconceptions from it is key in creating a good, and restful sleep.

Now, 7-8 hours of sleep is just average but it's not for everyone. Every person has different lengths of sleep where they feel well rested. Here are the three questions to assess whether the length and quality of your sleep is just fine:
  1. Do you feel really rested during the day?
  2. Can you not fall asleep involuntarily during the day?
  3. Do you generally fall asleep through the night without disturbances?

If you answer all three, then your length of sleep is just enough and suits you. If not, it is a very good time to pinpoint some hindrances keeping you from it? Here are key tips in improving your sleep:

  • Cool resting place - pre-cooling your bed is key in making you fall asleep faster
  • Silent and tranquil environment - find a place that you can fully relax away from any disturbances or noise
  • Keep away Phones and Gadgets in Bed - doing this in your bed creates an association with it to stay awake. This association ruins your intention of ever falling asleep in your bed. Make sure to make it as hard for you to bring your phone/gadgets in your bed. Locking it out in place, or simply putting your phone in bedtime mode and keeping it as far away from your bed will make you regain control of your association with your bed for resting.
  • Writing up in a Diary/Taking Note - sometimes our mind wanders around and makes us unsettle our thoughts. Simply journaling and taking note of these thoughts help in calming ourselves down, and we can go back to these thoughts in the morning when we are well-rested.
  • Therapy - If there are things way beyond the remedy recommended here, it is best to consult a health professional to help you get rested. A therapy has been proven to effectively help people with chronic trouble in falling asleep (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia).

Want to Easily Wake Up? Time your sleep.

Do you wanna easily wake up to your alarm? Then, with your length of sleep, consider the timing of when you sleep. A whole sleep cycle lasts for 1.5 hours, and this website, SleepoPolis, recommends some specific time when you should fall asleep. This timing accounts when your sleep cycle ends, so that when your alarm hits off, you will easily be awake. Do take note to have a 30 minutes before the recommended time so that by the recommended timing your sleep cycle is already starting.

Want a Relaxed Morning Wake Up Call? Choose your alarm wisely.

Have you ever dreaded or hated hearing your alarm? Maybe consider subtle alarm sounds that do not really put you on high alert. It is recommended that you use pleasing tones so that you do not activate your fight/flight mode. This mode is usually associated with panic, distress, and overall unpleasant feeling such as annoyance.
1.4. Exercising

It is recommended to have at least a total of 150 minutes of moderate activity per week. But breaking this number down, it doesn't have to be a single session, but can be 5 days with a 30-minute exercise, which makes this doable, and you can further modify it to your liking.

Here are types of exercises arranged at increasing difficulty and from which you can find what suits you. Remember, go at your own pace (~Adele).

Tai chi

Who said exercising has to be really intensive? This exercise was assigned to us in a school report, and it is recommended for people who prefer a type of meditative and light exercise. It is practiced through a controlled sequence of body movements specifically of the hands and feet. These are the two channels that I recommended to my mother when she was trying this herself: Taiflow and PeterChenTaiChiLife

Walking

Who knew? Just consistent everyday walks and this helps your body to stay healthy.

Yoga

I have attempted trying yoga but my joints have proven me otherwise. If you are already flexible yourself, yoga may fit just right for you. Although, I did not stick to this exercise, I incorporated some of it in my workout for my stretches and warm up. Here are some creators, I followed which helped me started it: BreatheandFlow and MadFit

Running/jogging

For each week, this is the app I have been using to time myself and track how far I have jogged. For each day, 5 times a week, I target at least 3km run; at an average I complete it in less than 30 minutes. [Running Tracker]

Workout

Perhaps, what comes first in mind when we talk of exercises are workout, and sometimes starting sudden and big goals is quite discouraging in keeping this routine long term.

The main key to ever maintaining not just working out but to every routine mentioned here: first start easy, doing doable and simple starts is key in keeping the ball rolling. Moreover, maintaining this routine on a day that you don't feel like doing your routine, know that there are days that this will happen away from enthusiastic motivation. Doing just a bit of it is key in creating continuous improvements. Know that you are doing your best, and each day's best is different from another. The main idea here is you don't have to fully deliver everyday, just do what you can. Because keeping these incremental changes is way more important than pressuring yourself with high unrealistic goals which you will just suddenly stop when you are discouraged from doing it.

Start from beginner exercise, and increment the difficulty of each exercise. Here is the app I have been using right now, Home Workouts

At first, it was hard starting in the beginner exercises, and just feels impossible to ever complete it. Now, I am on the advanced part of my workout and I have been enjoying it ever since I started to see my progression.
TL; DR (Too Long; Didn't Read.)

Self-love doesn't have to be elaborate nor complex. These are the tips to simply amp your self-care routine.

For your skincare routine, a cleanser, sunscreen and retinol routine will just do magic on you. Focus on active ingredients with your skincare products will be just as effective than having a long and detailed routine.

For showering, a quick 4-5 minute shower with focus on cleaning your groin and armpit area is just good enough in keeping your body clean. The Frequency of it highly depends on you, are you active? Going sweaty? Gets body odor easily? A quick shower will help you manage those.

For sleep, make sure you have a cool, quiet resting place, you can consider taking notes/journal to calm your thoughts, putting away your gadgets in your bed or notching up your alarm can all contribute to well-rested sleep.

For exercising, you can choose to start with either Tai-chi, walking, yoga, running/jogging or exercise. You can go at your own pace in completing a total 150 minutes of moderate physical activity per week.
